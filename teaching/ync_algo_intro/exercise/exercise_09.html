Exercise 9: Dynamic Programming


  1. Discuss the hallmarks (overlapping subproblems and optimal subproblems) and the key idea of dynamic programming. Provide examples in your discussion.
  2. Consider a modification of the rod-cutting problem in which, in addition to a price p_i for each rod, each cut incurs a fixed cost of c. The revenue associated with a solution is now the sum of the prices of the pieces minus the costs of making the cuts. Give a dynamic-programming algorithm to solve this modified problem.
  3. Modify MEMOIZED-CUT-ROD to return not only the value but the actual solution, too.
  4. The Fibonacci numbers are defined by recurrence. Give an O(n)-time dynamic-programming algorithm to compute the nth Fibonacci number. Draw the subproblem graph. How many vertices and edges are in the graph?
  5. Find an optimal parenthesization of a matrix-chain product whose sequence of dimensions is < 5, 10, 3, 12, 5, 50, 6 >
  6. Give a recursive algorithm MATRIX-CHAIN-MULTIPLY(A, s, i, j) that actually performs the optimal matrix-chain multiplication, given the sequence of matrices < A_1, A_2, ... ,A_n>, the ss table computed by MATRIX-CHAIN-ORDER, and the indices i and j. (The initial call would be MATRIX-CHAIN-MULTIPLY(A, s, 1, n).)
  7. Draw the recursion tree for the MERGE-SORT procedure on an array of 16 elements. Explain why memoization fails to speed up a good divide-and-conquer algorithm such as MERGE-SORT.
  8. Consider a variant of the matrix-chain multiplication problem in which the goal is to parenthesize the sequence of matrices so as to maximize, rather than minimize, the number of scalar multiplications. Does this problem exhibit optimal substructure?
